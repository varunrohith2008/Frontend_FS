Waterlogging and Flooding

Even moderate rainfall leads to severe waterlogging in several areas because of clogged stormwater drains and encroached water bodies. The city‚Äôs rapid urbanization has reduced natural drainage paths. As a result, residents face traffic chaos, property damage, and health risks during monsoons.

Encroachment of Footpaths

Many sidewalks in Chennai are occupied by parked vehicles, roadside vendors, and building ramps. This forces pedestrians to walk on busy roads, increasing the risk of accidents. Poor enforcement of civic rules and lack of pedestrian-focused planning worsen the issue.

Raise
Overcrowded Public Schools

Government schools in Chennai are struggling with overcrowded classrooms and inadequate facilities. Many students have to sit on the floor due to a shortage of benches and space. This affects both the quality of learning and the overall well-being of students.

Raise
Sewage Overflow and Poor Sanitation

In several localities, sewage overflow onto streets is common because of outdated or damaged drainage systems. Construction work often disrupts existing pipelines, and repairs are slow. The resulting unhygienic conditions cause foul smells, mosquito breeding, and diseases.

Infrastructure Work Disruptions

Ongoing metro and road projects cause long-term traffic diversions and dust pollution. While they promise better infrastructure later, residents currently deal with congestion, noise, and accessibility issues.
